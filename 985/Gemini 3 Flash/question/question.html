Telescoping Triangles

Consider a triangle $T_k$. Another triangle $T_{k+1}$ is constructed inside $T_k$ such that its three vertices lie one on each side of $T_k$. For each side of $T_k$, the angles formed between it and the two sides of $T_{k+1}$ it touches are equal to each other.

For some triangles $T_0$, this process can be repeated to create a sequence of triangles $T_0, T_1, T_2, \dots$. However, for other triangles, the process may terminate. We say that $T_k$ exists if it is a triangle and its vertices lie on the sides of $T_{k-1}$ as described.

Suppose $T_0$ has side lengths $(8, 9, 10)$. Then $T_1$ and $T_2$ exist, but $T_3$ does not. Amongst all integer-sided triangles $T_0$ such that $T_2$ exists but $T_3$ does not exist, the smallest possible perimeter is $10$ when $T_0$ has side lengths $(3, 3, 4)$.

Find the smallest possible perimeter of an integer-sided triangle $T_0$ such that $T_{20}$ exists but $T_{21}$ does not exist.
